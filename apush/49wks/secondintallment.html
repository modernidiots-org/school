Cold War Notecards

Affluence & Anxiety

Taft-Hartley Act (1/580)
1947 law limiting union power. Allowed states to ban union shops. Required union leaders to deny communism. Weakened labor.
McCarran Internal Security Act (2/581)
Required communist groups to register. Allowed detention during emergencies. Increased anti-communist measures. Reflected Cold War fears.
G.I. Bill (3/582)
Provided benefits to WWII veterans. Funded education and housing. Expanded middle class and boosted economy.
HUAC (4/583)
House committee investigating communism. Targeted Hollywood and government. Led to blacklists and increased fear.
United Nations (5/584)
International peace organization formed after WWII. Replaced League of Nations. Promoted cooperation.
Korean War (6/585)
Conflict between North and South Korea. U.S. fought under UN. Ended in stalemate and reinforced containment.
Rosenbergs (7/586)
Couple convicted of spying for USSR. Executed in 1953. Increased Cold War fears.
Containment (George Kennan) (8/587)
Policy to stop spread of communism. Guided Cold War strategy and influenced Truman Doctrine.
Joseph McCarthy (9/588)
Senator leading anti-communist crusade. Made false accusations and created hysteria before being censured.
38th Parallel (10/589)
Line dividing North and South Korea. Became Cold War boundary and site of conflict.
Truman Doctrine (13/592)
Policy supporting countries resisting communism. Marked start of containment and expanded U.S. role.
U-2 Incident (14/593)
U.S. spy plane shot down over USSR. Increased tensions and damaged diplomacy.
CIA (16/595)
Agency conducting espionage and covert operations. Key Cold War tool.
NATO (20/599)
Military alliance against USSR. Promoted collective defense among Western nations.
Marshall Plan (21/600)
U.S. aid program rebuilding Europe. Prevented communism and strengthened alliances.
Domino Theory (28/607)
Belief that one country falling to communism leads others to fall. Influenced U.S. foreign policy.
Sputnik (29/608)
First satellite launched by USSR. Started space race and shocked Americans.
Mutually Assured Destruction (31/610)
Nuclear deterrence strategy where both sides could destroy each other, preventing direct war.
Berlin Airlift (33/612)
U.S. supplied West Berlin by air during Soviet blockade. Avoided war and symbolized resistance.
Brown v. Board of Education (34/613)
Supreme Court case ending school segregation. Major civil rights victory.
Mao Zedong (36/615)
Leader of Communist China. Major Cold War figure aligned with USSR.
Warsaw Pact (37/616)
Soviet military alliance countering NATO.
H-Bomb (40/619)
Hydrogen bomb more powerful than atomic bomb. Increased arms race.
Baby Boom (44/623)
Post-WWII population increase. Expanded suburbs and consumer demand.
Interstate Highway Act (48/627)
Funded highway system. Boosted transportation, defense, and suburbanization.
Little Rock Crisis (52/631)
Federal troops enforced school integration. Showed federal authority.
MLK & SCLC (53/632)
Civil rights leadership promoting nonviolent protest and equality.
Suburbanization (62/641)
Growth of suburbs driven by GI Bill and highways. Expanded middle-class lifestyle.
